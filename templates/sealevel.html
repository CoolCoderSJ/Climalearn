Higher sea levels means damaging flooding ocurring more frequently and more natural disasters. The National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) predicts that sea levels will rise as much as they did previously in 100 years now in only 30 years. (Source) Sea levels usually rise because of climate change. Find out what you can do to help slow this down.

Cause(s): Sea levels rise due to heat emitted from greenhouse gases exapnding the oceans and the increased melting of ice structures, like glaciers. Greenhouse gases come from places like motor vehicles that use fossil fuels as their fuel, and the burning of fossil fuels.

Effect(s): Floods will occur more frequently, and will be more damaging. Rising sea levels also affect homes near the coastlines, since they will be damaged by hazardous storms. According to the UN, over 80% of the world's largest cities are near a coast.
